About SGEM

SGEM (Stockholm Genetically Engineered Machines) is a non-profit association founded by alumni in 2015. Our mission is to support students in Stockholm participating in the iGEM (International Genetically Engineered Machine) Competition. We provide the structure, continuity, and network that enables each new team to build on the experience of the previous one. As the organisation grows, we aim to build stronger links between emerging talent and the life science sector across Stockholm and Sweden.

iGEM Competition

The iGEM (International Genetically Engineered Machine) Competition is the world's largest synthetic biology competition. Each year, student teams from around the world design and build innovative biological solutions to solve real-world problems. Their projects integrate biology, engineering, and computational methods to address challenges in health, sustainability, food systems, and the environment. The teams then present their work at the Grand Jamboree (in Paris), where they showcase their projects and compete with teams globally.

Team Stockholm

Each year, SGEM recruits students from universities across Stockholm to form the iGEM Stockholm team. Participating in the team offers a unique opportunity to work on a fully student-driven project while gaining hands-on experience in research, collaboration, and innovation.

Activities

Every year a new team. Every year a stronger foundation.

SGEM provides the structure, continuity, and network that connect generations of students in the Stockholm iGEM community.

Recruitment

The cycle begins with a new team.

Each year, we bring together motivated and curious students from diverse academic backgrounds to build a strong, balanced iGEM team.

Connection

Strong teams grow through trust.

In an open and inclusive environment, we help the team thrive, connect, and build team spirit.

Mentorship

Strong ideas build on experience.

Through workshops and discussions, we guide the team in developing their ideas and refining their strategy.

Structure

Maintenance eases the transition.

We provide the legal and administrative framework behind the project, ensuring finances, formal responsibilities, and long-term continuity.

Foundation

The foundation grows stronger with every team.

By connecting students, alumni, researchers, and partners, we preserve knowledge and support new teams year after year.

Expansion

Reaching outward strengthens what's already there.

Through outreach and external connections, we build on existing resources, partnerships, and opportunities that further support the team.
